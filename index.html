Bio
I am a software engineer and researcher specializing in embedded platforms, operating systems, and AI-driven system software at Samsung Electronics. My work focuses on optimizing low-level system components for consumer electronics and IoT devices, including smartphones, TVs, and edge systems. I have strong expertise in Linux kernel internals, memory management, runtime scheduling, and on-device AI. I have contributed to major open-source projects such as Linux, GCC, Glibc, Android, and Tizen, and have shared my work as a speaker and reviewer at conferences like OLS, LCA, ACM/IEEE ICSE, and ACM MobiCom. My research has appeared in top-tier venues such as IEEE T-CE, ACM EuroSys, and ACM/IEEE ICSE, and I have received awards including the IEEE Chester Sall Award and multiple Samsung Best Paper Awards. I also hold several patents related to system optimization and embedded AI deployment. I am passionate about advancing intelligent edge computing and embedded AI, building practical solutions that combine system software and AI to power the next generation of devices
